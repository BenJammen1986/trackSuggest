At Epicodus, we believe that every person:


  • Has the ability to learn programming, and
  • Should be given the opportunity to learn programming if they so desire-
    regardless of their background or financial situation.

That is why all our materials are available online for free!

With that being said:


It's important for YOU to make sure that you fully understand:

  1. The commitment that you are making (time, money, energy, etc...)
  2. The type of mindset it takes to become a successful developer.

If you are just looking at Indeed or Monster or Careerbuilder and see all the opportunities/income potential within programming - and there ARE many opportunities available - you may need to take a step back and re-evaluate.

We hope you understand: We aren't trying to discourage you!

On the contrary, we simply believe that it's important for you to have a BIG WHY: to know exactly why you've chosen to make this change in your life, and your purpose/goals that you want to accomplish once you finish. For most people, their driving motivation for completing a challenging, monumental task has to be a little deeper than simply "I can make XYZ dollars doing it"! That's probably not going to get you through the intensity of this program.

If you choose to come to Epicodus, we want you to be completely committed and focused, so that you can finish well and not only be successful in graduating, but be thoroughly satisfied with the career you've chosen!

So: your first step in your journey is to go through the questionnaire below and answer the questions as honestly as you can.

Is Programming for Me/Commitment Questionnaire:



1. How well do you work with others?

As a developer, you will likely be working with a group of other people. How comfortable are you working on a team?



2. Are you an active problem solver OR do you strongly want to develop your problem solving ability?

Developers MUST have or be willing to grow their problem solving abilities. Will you be able to persevere through difficult challenges?
Are you ok with "not knowing"- but have the initiative to find out for yourself?



3. A 6-month boot camp is a BIG commitment:

Are you ready to give up 6 months of your life? Can you make ends meet financially while going to school full-time?

If you are brand new to programming- you may need to study quite a bit, even outside of our rigorous school hours. Are you willing to sacrifice other non-essentials (tv, games, events/parties) to focus on this program?



4. What are your goals? What is your Why?

Students with a strong reason- a why, purpose, and goals- have a far better chance of finishing our program and succeeding in their programming career;
those without it have a tendency to burn out.

Do you have strong goals and a big why?



5. Do you have the right personality to work in this environment?

While there are all kinds of people who are successful programmers, there are certain traits that are common with them:

  • Would you be comfortable working on a computer in an office 8-10 hours a day?
  • Can you stay focused on a single task for long periods of time during the day- and projects for possibly months or years?
  • Would you enjoy/be willing to deal with debugging, reformatting, and properly noting or correcting your work?

Congratulations!

It looks like you may be a good fit for one of our programming tracks!


Just as it is important to make sure that you have the right personality to work in the programming world in general- it is also a good idea to gauge what kind of work environment you will thrive in:

  • Would you like to work with a large company with more structure?
  • Or rather: smaller teams, risk/reward of startups?
  • Are you more geared towards design (front end) or function/logic (back end programming)?
